MY HOMETOWN

"MUNSHIGANJ"

History:

Munshiganj, formerly known as Bikrampur, is a historically significant region of Bangladesh. It was once the capital of the Sena Dynasty and a center of Hindu-Buddhist learning, with scholars like Atish Dipankar born here. During the Mughal period, Islamic culture spread across the area. Under British rule, the region was renamed Munshiganj and became known for zamindars and its role in political awareness. In the 1971 Liberation War, many locals fought bravely, and several memorials stand in their honor. In 1984, Munshiganj became a separate district. Today, it is known for its rich heritage, Padma River, and famous hilsa fish.

Geography:

Munshiganj district, located in Dhaka Division, Bangladesh, is characterized by its fertile land and numerous rivers, including the Padma, Meghna, Dhaleshwari, and Ichamati. It's bounded by Dhaka and Narayanganj to the north, Madaripur and Shariatpur to the south, Comilla and Chandpur to the east, and Dhaka and Faridpur to the west. The district spans 1004.29 sq km and is known for its agricultural production, particularly potatoes. The district is divided into 6 upazilas (sub-districts): Gazaria, Tongibari, Munshiganj Sadar, Lohajang, Sreenagar, and Serajdikhan. Munshiganj Sadar Upazila, the main urban center, has two municipalities and nine union.

Famous spots :

Munshiganj, rich in history and natural beauty, offers several notable tourist attractions. One of the most famous is Idrakpur Fort, a 17th-century Mughal structure built to protect against pirate attacks and river invasions. Nearby, the Sonarang Twin Temples stand tall, showcasing stunning Hindu temple architecture. Another remarkable site is the ancient Baba Adam’s Mosque, known for its terracotta designs and spiritual value. History lovers often visit the Atish Dipankar Memorial, dedicated to the renowned Buddhist scholar born in this region. The old Munshiganj Rajbari reflects colonial charm and zamindari heritage. Nature enthusiasts are drawn to the scenic Padma River banks, especially at Mawa Ghat, where boat rides and fresh hilsa fish are local highlights. Additionally, the Baligaon Baor, a natural wetland, offers peaceful surroundings, fishing, and birdwatching. These spots together make Munshiganj a place of both cultural richness and serene natural beauty.

Culinary Heritage of Munshiganj:

Munshiganj is widely known for its rich and traditional food culture, especially its river-fresh dishes and unique sweets. The district is famous for ilish (hilsa fish), freshly caught from the Padma and Meghna rivers. Local specialties like shorshe ilish (hilsa in mustard curry) and bhuna ilish are must-tries. Among sweets, spong mishti (sponge sweets) is especially popular for its soft, juicy texture, and patkhir, a rare and traditional sweet made from milk, is a local delicacy found only in this region. Other well-loved items include balish mishti, chhanar polao, and winter-time pithas like patishapta and bhapa pitha. At Mawa Ghat, visitors often enjoy hot rice with fried hilsa while watching the river — a classic Munshiganj experience. These diverse and flavorful dishes make Munshiganj a memorable destination for food lovers.
